by Deepti Pradeep¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

Prosper loans provides loans to borrower's who need loans. Their interest rates vary on the creditworthy on the borrowers and the information the borrower's provide in their application form. The dataset available here has 113937 records. It is interesting to understand the basis of fixing the borrower's interest rate.

This dataset was directly available on Udacity server and was downloaded to my local computer to carry out the analysis and visualization

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The dataset contains 113937 records with 61 columns after dropping 20 columns that I felt were not useful for the visualisation.

The dataset most contains floats, with few columns with int, bool and object datatypes.The object datatypes contains nominal categorical datatypes. For example, EmploymentStatus is a nominal categorical datatype with values such as 'Employed', 'Part-Time', 'Full-Time', and few more.

There are also ordinal categorical data such as ProsperRating (numeric) reprsented as float datatype. The numbers represent 0- NA, 1- HR, 2 - E,3 - D, 4 - C,5 - B,6 - A,7 - AA where higher number represents best and lower number represents worst.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

The main idea is to find out more about the BorrowerRate or the borrower's interest rate on the loan.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

There are a few parameters such as Occupation, EmploymentStatus, ProsperRating, ProsperScore, Term, CreditScoreLower and CreditScoreUpper that may impact the BorrowerRate.
